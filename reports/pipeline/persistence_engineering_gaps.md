# PERSEUS engineering-gap research (deep-research workflow wf_6845bfe2)

Four highest-ROI engineering-gap reports, recovered from the workflow journal. Web-grounded; spot-check citations before relying on specifics.

## The durable-plasticity targets split cleanly into two tractability classes for a sequence-only DTI model like MAMMAL, and the project's existing `persistence_dti.py` module already proves this empirically — but it only covers ONE of the four mechanistic nodes and treats it correctly-but-pessimistically. (1) SCOREABLE-IN-PRINCIPLE by sequence: nothing in this list is cleanly scoreable as a *durability* signal, because every durable node is either an allosteric/quaternary site or intracellular-access-gated. The closest to tractable is AMPA (GRIA1, P42261) and intracellular 5-HT2A (HTR2A, P28223) viewed purely as ligand-binding events — both are already in the repo's seeds — BUT for both, binding is necessary-not-sufficient and MAMMAL's BindingDB-pKd head is documented to be allosterically blind (the v1 audit measured CHRNA7 PAM std=0.029, near-flat), so the very thing that makes AMPA potentiation *durable* (PAM-driven, GluA1-phosphorylation-gated, mTORC1-coupled late-phase) is invisible to it. (2) REQUIRES-3D / INTRACELLULAR-ACCESS-GATED (sequence model fundamentally cannot represent): TrkB transmembrane-domain binding is the canonical case — the antidepressant/ketamine site is a crossed-dimer wedge in the TMD that depends on membrane cholesterol concentration and only forms in the lipid bilayer (Casarotto 2021 Cell; Cordeiro/Nat Commun 2024 solved it by NMR-in-lipid, not by a static apo structure), so a model fed a 1D amino-acid sequence with no membrane, no dimer partner, and no cholesterol has zero information channel for it. Intracellular 5-HT2A is gated by a DIFFERENT axis entirely — it is not a binding-pocket problem but a PHYSICOCHEMICAL/COMPARTMENT problem: the receptor is the same protein, but plasticity requires the ligand to cross the lipid membrane (lipophilicity/LogP, demonstrated by the N-methylation/LogP series and the SERT-rescue and electroporation-rescue experiments in Vargas 2023 Science). MAMMAL's DTI head will give serotonin and DMT near-identical pKd at HTR2A because they ARE near-identical orthosteric binders; the durable-plasticity difference lives in membrane permeability, which is an ADMET-axis property (the project already has ADMET-AI BBB/Caco-2/logP for exactly this). mTORC1/BDNF cascade is not a drug TARGET at all in the psychoplastogen mechanism — it is the DOWNSTREAM effector (the "growth phase" requiring sustained mTOR+AMPA after the initial TrkB "stimulation phase," Ly 2021 ACS Pharmacol Transl Sci), so scoring a psychoplastogen's affinity for MTOR/RPTOR is a category error; mTORC1 belongs in the transcriptomic/pathway axis (Cluster D / LINCS), not the DTI panel. NET GAP: the existing persistence module encodes TrkB as a `plasticity_window` tier that (correctly) never auto-promotes to durable, and its MW-residualized calibration is the single most important under-exploited result in the repo — after de-confounding molecular weight, the two 'passing' senolytic channels (BCL2 resid-AUROC 0.46, BCL2L1 0.32) COLLAPSE as size artifacts, while NTRK2/TrkB is the ONLY target *rescued* (resid-AUROC 0.815, all engagers in-MW-range so the residualization is honest not extrapolated). Yet the panel has no AMPA-PAM channel, no intracellular-access (lipophilicity) gate distinguishing membrane-permeant from impermeant 5-HT2A agonists, and no explicit firewall preventing MTOR/downstream nodes from ever being treated as DTI targets. The persistence module is essentially a single-node (TrkB) plasticity reader masquerading as a four-node one.

The four molecular mediators of DURABLE psychoplastogen effects are not one tractability class — they are four, and the project's existing `src/mammal_repurposing/engine/persistence_dti.py` already demonstrates the split. VERIFIED MECHANISM (six primary sources): durable structural plasticity proceeds in two epochs — an initial TrkB-dependent STIMULATION phase (as short as 15 min of drug exposure) followed by a self-sustaining GROWTH phase requiring sustained mTOR + AMPA-receptor activation; ANA-12/TrkB block works only during stimulation, rapamycin/mTOR and DNQX/AMPA block both phases; growth then continues 18-71 h after drug washout, and a single ketamine dose sustains effects 10-14 days despite a ~2-3 h half-life (Ly 2021 ACSPTS; Autry/Monteggia rapid-AD literature). TRACTABILITY VERDICT for a sequence-based DTI model: (a) TrkB-TMD binding — NOT sequence-scoreable; the antidepressant/ketamine site is a crossed transmembrane-dimer wedge gated by membrane cholesterol, abolished by the single Y433F mutation in the CARC motif, and was only solvable by NMR-in-lipid (Casarotto 2021 Cell; Cordeiro 2024 Nat Commun). A 1D sequence carries no membrane, no dimer, no cholesterol context, so the durability-relevant binding mode is information-theoretically absent. (b) Intracellular 5-HT2A — NOT gated by the binding pocket (which IS sequence-visible) but by ligand LIPOPHILICITY/membrane permeability; serotonin and psychedelics bind 5-HT2A near-identically, but only membrane-permeant agonists reach the intracellular pool that drives growth (Vargas 2023 Science, via N-methylation/LogP, SERT-rescue, electroporation-rescue). This is an ADMET-axis property the repo already computes (ADMET-AI BBB/logP/Caco-2), NOT a DTI property. (c) AMPA (GRIA1) — orthosteric binding is sequence-visible, but the durability lever is allosteric potentiation (PAM) + GluA1 phosphorylation + mTORC1 coupling, and MAMMAL is documented allosterically blind (v1 CHRNA7 PAM std=0.029). (d) mTORC1/BDNF cascade — a DOWNSTREAM EFFECTOR, not a psychoplastogen drug target; scoring affinity for MTOR/RPTOR is a category error — it belongs in the transcriptomic/pathway axis (LINCS/Cluster D). EMPIRICAL CONFIRMATION already in-repo: in `data/results/persistence_dti_calibration.json` TrkB/NTRK2 FAILS raw calibration (AUROC 0.717, perm-p 0.087, sensitivity 0.0 at the specificity-first threshold); but in `data/results/persistence_dti_calibration_mwresid.json`, after molecular-weight residualization, the two senolytic channels collapse (BCL2 0.46, BCL2L1 0.32 — they were size artifacts) and TrkB is the SOLE rescued channel (resid-AUROC 0.815, engager_in_range_frac 1.0). So MAMMAL CAN rank true TrkB engagers above matched non-engagers once size is removed — but it still cannot tell a durable TMD-wedge binder (fluoxetine/ketamine) from an intracellular ATP-site kinase inhibitor (larotrectinib), because the current NTRK2 anchor set conflates three incompatible sites (extracellular 7,8-DHF, intracellular ATP-pocket TRK inhibitors, and zero TMD antidepressants). The honest framing the module already uses — TrkB as a non-auto-promoting `plasticity_window`, ablative/senolysis as the only durable-by-construction tier — is correct and should be preserved.

**Concrete next step:** Build a "psychoplastogen-persistence" extension to the EXISTING persistence module (do NOT start a new pipeline) as a 3-channel, mechanism-typed module, gating each channel by what MAMMAL can actually represent. Step 1 (data, ~1 day): create `data/raw/psychoplastogen_targets_seed.csv` adding two channels alongside the existing NTRK2 plasticity_window row — GRIA1/P42261 tier=`plasticity_window` (already has sequence + an allosteric_benchmark.csv PAM/orthosteric label set to reuse) and HTR2A/P28223 tier=`plasticity_window` (already in targets_liability_seed.csv + modulator_anchors_seed.csv). Step 2 (the load-bearing fix, ~1 day): SPLIT the NTRK2 anchor set in `persistence_dti_anchors_seed.csv` by binding SITE — tmd_wedge (fluoxetine, imipramine, ketamine, R,R-HNK; the durability-relevant site), ecd (7,8-DHF), atp_pocket (larotrectinib/entrectinib/selitrectinib) — and calibrate TrkB ONLY against the tmd_wedge engagers, so the per-target AUROC measures the question that matters; pre-register the expectation that MAMMAL FAILS the tmd_wedge calibration (this is a publishable negative result that directly extends the v1 allosteric-blindness audit and the existing MW-residual finding). Step 3 (intracellular-access gate, ~half day): add a function in `persistence_dti.py` that, for any HTR2A engagement, REQUIRES a lipophilicity/membrane-permeability pass from the already-built ADMET-AI cluster (logP and Caco-2/BBB) before the 5-HT2A channel may contribute — encoding the Vargas 2023 finding that the binding score is the same for permeant/impermeant agonists and permeability is the discriminator. This is a cross-cluster gate, not a new DTI score. Step 4 (firewall, trivial): add an explicit assertion/registry that MTOR, RPTOR, RICTOR, MLST8 and other downstream-effector UniProts are FORBIDDEN as DTI panel targets, with a docstring pointing mTORC1/BDNF-cascade scoring to the transcriptomic axis (Cluster D / LINCS) — preventing the category error. Step 5 (the durability semantics, ~half day): keep the existing tier logic exactly — only ablative/senolytic auto-promotes to durable; the three psychoplastogen channels (TrkB/AMPA/5-HT2A) emit a combined `plasticity_window` hypothesis that fires ONLY when (TrkB-tmd OR membrane-permeant-5HT2A) co-occurs with the AMPA channel, mirroring the literature's two-epoch stimulation+growth requirement, and is reported as a window/capability flag, never as durable-by-construction. Validation gate: the module should classify ketamine and a permeant psychedelic (DMT/psilocin) as plasticity-window-positive and serotonin / an impermeant analog as window-negative DESPITE near-identical 5-HT2A pKd — if MAMMAL's raw DTI alone (no ADMET gate) cannot achieve that separation, that proves the intracellular-access axis is non-DTI and the gate is doing the work. All five steps reuse existing sequences, anchors, calibration math (auroc/permutation_p/mw_residualize/specificity_threshold), and the ADMET cluster; net new code is one CSV, one anchor-split, one cross-cluster gate function, and one forbidden-target assertion.

**ROI:** HIGH ROI, and unusually so because ~80% of the infrastructure already exists and the highest-value output is a rigorous NEGATIVE result rather than a speculative positive. (3) It prevents two expensive category errors before they cost wet-lab money: scoring MTOR/RPTOR as a psychoplastogen DTI target (the cascade is downstream, not a target), and trusting a high 5-HT2A pKd as a plasticity signal when permeant and impermeant agonists are isoaffine — both are exactly the kind of false positive the v10/v11 target-collapse and peptide-pollution post-mortems show this pipeline is prone to. RISK/CAVEAT: this does not yield a new durable-plasticity drug prediction (the durability signal is genuinely not in MAMMAL's modality), so if the goal is a positive repurposing hit the ROI is low; the high ROI is specifically for methodological rigor, avoiding false positives, and a defensible negative-result publication consistent with the project's honesty discipline.

## PERSEUS L1 Stage-3 (efflux-aware free-brain exposure) currently hard-returns ABSTAIN: there is no fitted Kp,uu regressor and no conformal applicability band, so the only CNS gate the engine actually exercises is a passive-penetration physicochemical window (Wager CNS-MPO-like + permanent-charge/peptide vetoes in src/mammal_repurposing/engine/cns_exposure.py). That window cannot distinguish "crosses the BBB passively" (high logBB/Pe) from "achieves free brain exposure at the target" (Kp,uu >= ~0.3), which is exactly the efflux-dominated distinction that matters for cognition targets. The deeper field-level gap, web-verified across 4 independent sources: PUBLIC measured rat Kp,uu data is tiny and historically undisclosed, and even the best efflux-aware models plateau at modest accuracy (Fridén PLS Q2~0.45; AstraZeneca-style efflux-as-variable R2~0.53; Takeda GP R2~0.60 internal / ~0.48 external; rat D-MPNN+PBPK R2~0.30) — so the honest deliverable is NOT a high-R2 regressor but a well-calibrated point estimate PLUS a conformal interval that abstains widely out-of-domain, which no public CNS predictor currently ships for Kp,uu.

Best PUBLIC datasets (web-verified June 2026): (1) B3DB (theochem/B3DB) — 7,982 BBB+/- classification + 1,058 NUMERIC logBB compounds, compiled from 50 sources, CC0 license, on GitHub + PyPI (qc-B3DB) + HuggingFace (maomlab/B3DB), mordred descriptors precomputed; CAVEAT: logBB (TOTAL brain/plasma), NOT Kp,uu — use as large auxiliary/pretraining + applicability-domain anchor, not as Kp,uu ground truth. (2) TDC BBB_Martins — 1,975 compounds, classification only, AUROC leaderboard, permissive loader; this is what ADMET-AI's BBB head (already integrated in this repo) is trained on. (3) Fridén et al. 2009 (J Med Chem) — canonical PUBLIC measured rat Kp,uu,brain set, n=41 train (+~145 external), homogenate/brain-slice/microdialysis; PLS Q2~0.45; the de-facto benchmark seed every later study reuses. (4) Morales et al. 2024 (Front Drug Discov) — DISCLOSED Kp,uu set, 157 compounds (refined P-gp/BCRP-excluded subset n=67), XGBoost 85.1% acc / AUROC 0.891 (classification framing); explicitly notes most prior Kp,uu datasets were "totally or partially undisclosed." (5) CMD-FGKpuu 2025 (JCIM 65:5099, github.com/zyrlia1018/CMD-FGKpuu) — FIRST public DATA-MINED rat Kp,uu dataset + formula-guided/symbolic-regression DL model with a fine-tune path; repo has descriptor-calc + symbolic-regression + model + fine_tune/predict dirs; CRITICAL CAVEAT: repo has NO LICENSE file (all-rights-reserved by default) — email authors for reuse rights before vendoring, important since this project hardens for open-source. Large efflux-aware Kp,uu datasets (AstraZeneca/Varadharajan ~1,000; Takeda 640; Spreafico physics-based) are IN-HOUSE / not public. LEADING METHODS: (a) efflux-as-explicit-variable "model-within-a-model" — predict an in-vitro MDR1/BCRP efflux ratio (or P-gp-substrate probability) from structure, then feed it as a feature into the Kp,uu regressor (Varadharajan/AZ: adding the P-gp variable lifted R2 0.53, RMSE 0.57, kappa 0.6, using an in-vitro set 4x larger than the in-vivo set); (b) gradient-boosted trees (XGBoost/LightGBM) or Gaussian Processes on RDKit + mordred 2D descriptors are SOTA for this small-data/noisy regime (GP gave best internal R2 0.60 at Takeda; GP also natively yields a variance); (c) D-MPNN graph nets (Chemprop-RDKit = ADMET-AI; Swanson 2024 Bioinformatics, top average rank on TDC ADMET leaderboard, pip-installable, CC-licensed Zenodo weights) for the BBB-classification leg and as a passive-permeability prior; (d) physicochemical priors CNS-MPO (Wager 2010, already in repo) and CNS-BBB score as cheap features/sanity gates. CONFORMAL APPLICABILITY BAND: crepes (Boström, MLR 2022/2024; the R1 task already names it) is the right tool — model-agnostic, supports NORMALIZED (difficulty-weighted) and MONDRIAN conformal regressors/predictive systems, so you can (i) get distribution-free PI coverage on log Kp,uu and (ii) bin Mondrian categories by chemotype or by predicted-efflux so coverage holds per-subpopulation; MAPIE is the fallback. Field reality that justifies abstention-first design: even efflux-aware rat Kp,uu models plateau at R2~0.3-0.6 because data are scarce and efflux-dominated, so the conformal interval (and a "predicted P-gp substrate -> widen band / ABSTAIN" rule) IS the deliverable.

The verified literature says the state-vs-tone axis the engine encodes is correct in spirit but under-resolved on three structure/target-derivable signature families it does not exploit, plus it carries one assumption the 2025 literature contradicts. (1) NO immediate-early-gene (IEG) axis: Arc/Fos/Egr1/Zif268/Npas4/BDNF-exonIV are the gate between transient activation and durable consolidation, but IEG induction is necessary-not-sufficient and shows tolerance on repeated stimulant dosing, so it is a durability ENABLER not a guarantee; it is also an assay readout, only weakly structure-derivable. ## PERSEUS currently reports only SPECIFICITY (over claim rate 0 of 14 plus a coverage accuracy curve) because the durable in healthy positive class is near empty. persistence_eval.py lines 17 to 20 explicitly declare sensitivity and PPV unidentifiable and substitute a Wald label budget (about 381 positives at a 1 percent prior). The gap: once a small 10 to 30 verified positive set exists, how to honestly measure sensitivity and PPV given (i) a near zero base rate that makes class prior estimation ill posed, and (ii) verified positives that are almost certainly selection biased (SAR not SCAR) because they were verified precisely by having rare gold standard delayed start evidence. The current label_budget Wald formula is also the wrong small sample tool.

The four requested 2018 to 2026 literatures divide the labor cleanly and map closely onto perseus_design.md's existing sketch (PU plus leave one mechanism out plus Saerens prior correction plus conformal plus coverage accuracy). (1) PU performance recovery: Jain, White and Radivojac (NeurIPS 2017, arXiv 1702.00518) recover ROC and PR curves from PU data given the class prior; Ramola, Jain and Radivojac (PSB 2019, PMC6417800) give closed form corrections of precision, recall, F1, accuracy and MCC from alpha (prior in unlabeled) and beta (1 minus label noise). KEY CAVEAT: these rest on SCAR (positives labeled independent of features). A set verified BY having rare delayed start trials violates SCAR and is SAR (Bekker and Davis survey, Machine Learning 2020, arXiv 1811.04820), needing propensity or IPS or SAR EM, or PULSNAR (arXiv 2303.08269, 2023, peer reviewed 2024) which clusters non SCAR positive subtypes. (2) Class prior estimation is provably ill posed absent an irreducibility assumption (Blanchard 2010; Jain 2016); practical estimators AlphaMax (Jain 2016), KM and KM2 RKHS (Ramaswamy 2016), Pearson divergence (du Plessis and Sugiyama 2014) are high variance at a 1 percent base rate, so the prior should be an externally supplied interval, not estimated from the data. TCPU (Mielniczuk, Rejchel and Teisseyre 2025, arXiv 2502.21194) extends prior estimation to the label shift case with non asymptotic bounds. (3) Prior corrected calibration under label shift: Saerens, Latinne and Decaestecker (Neural Computation 2002) EM adjusts posteriors to a new prior; BBSE (Lipton 2018) and MLLS plus bias corrected calibration (Alexandari, Kundaje and Shrikumar, ICML 2020, arXiv 1901.06852) re express metrics at the realistic deployment prior instead of the enriched eval prior. (4) Conformal under label shift: weighted conformal (Tibshirani, Barber, Candes and Ramdas, NeurIPS 2019, arXiv 1904.06019); label shift conformal plus recalibration (Podkopaev and Ramdas, UAI 2021, arXiv 2103.03323) reweight by the per class prior ratio so prediction sets and coverage stay valid when eval prior differs from deployment prior. (5) Small sample CIs: with 10 to 30 verified positives, sensitivity is one binomial proportion; Brown, Cai and DasGupta (Statistical Science 2001) and the rare event CI work (arXiv 2109.02516) say use Wilson or Jeffreys, NOT Wald (which is what the current label_budget encodes), and report margin of error relative to the small proportion.

**Concrete next step:** Add a persistence_pu_eval.py companion to persistence_eval.py, on the new 10 to 30 verified positive ledger, with mechanism class as the grouping unit throughout (grouped leave one mechanism out per perseus_design lines 119 to 121, to block chemotype memorization). (A) Sensitivity is the one directly identifiable quantity: score the verified positives, count how many clear the PERSEUS persistence assert gate at each operating threshold, and report recall as a binomial proportion with a Jeffreys or Wilson 95 percent interval (replace the Wald label_budget math at persistence_eval.py lines 88 to 95; at n equals 10 to 30 the CI is roughly plus or minus 0.18 to 0.30 wide, reported honestly as the headline precision limit). (B) PPV is NOT a point estimate at this base rate: report it as a curve over an externally supplied prior interval pi in 0.005 to 0.03 via PPV equals pi times sens divided by (pi times sens plus (1 minus pi) times FPR), taking FPR from the existing reversible enhancer and persistence illusion control panels, delivering the perseus_design line 127 to 128 framing at X percent abstention, Y PPV at the realistic 1 percent prior. (C) Apply the Ramola Jain Radivojac 2019 PU correction ONLY as a sensitivity analysis, gated behind an explicit SCAR vs SAR check (compare the verified positive score distribution to the unlabeled pool); if biased, switch to the SAR or PULSNAR path or report the correction as a bound, not a number. (D) Wrap the operating point in Podkopaev Ramdas 2021 label shift reweighted conformal so the asserted persistence set carries a coverage guarantee transported from the enriched eval prior to the 1 percent deployment prior. Keep the existing coverage accuracy curve and 0 over claim specificity as co headline. Net honest claim: sensitivity X (Jeffreys 95 percent CI lo to hi) at FPR at or below f on the control panels; PPV ranges P_lo to P_hi across prior in 0.5 to 3 percent, which is reportable at n equals 10 to 30 and is exactly what no current repurposing persistence predictor publishes.

**ROI:** HIGH ROI, low effort. This is the single named blocker on the project's deepest deliverable: persistence_eval.py and perseus_design.md both flag that sensitivity and PPV need a non empty positive ledger plus a PU / leave one mechanism out estimator as THE next deliverable, and GAPS_AND_RESEARCH_DIRECTIONS F8 calls it out explicitly. The build is about 1 to 2 days of numpy and sklearn (no new infra, no GPU): a Jeffreys/Wilson recall CI, a PPV vs prior curve over the EXISTING negative control panels, and a grouped leave one mechanism out loop reusing the current ground truth ledger. The literature is mature and directly transferable. Most importantly it converts an honest non result (unidentifiable, label budget about 381) into a publishable, reviewer defensible bidirectional metric (sensitivity X with CI; PPV range across realistic prior) which the design doc notes no current repurposing persistence predictor reports. The chief risk is scientific, not engineering: the 10 to 30 verified positives are almost certainly SAR (selection biased by trial availability), so the deliverable's integrity depends on running the SCAR vs SAR diagnostic FIRST and reporting PU corrected numbers as bounds, not points, consistent with the project house rule of honest negatives over forced positives.
